Law And Rights



1.Law is a set of rules that are created and are enforceable by social or governmental institutions to regulate behavior, with its precise definition a matter of longstanding debate.

2.It has been variously described as a science and as the art of justice.

3.State-enforced laws can be made by a group legislature or by a single legislator, resulting in statutes; by the executive through decrees and regulations; or established by judges through precedent, usually in common law jurisdictions.

4.Private individuals may create legally binding contracts, including arbitration agreements that adopt alternative ways of resolving disputes to standard court litigation.

5.The creation of laws themselves may be influenced by a constitution, written or tacit, and the rights encoded therein.
